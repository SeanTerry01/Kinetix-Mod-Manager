# Stardew Valley Accessible Mod Manager
## The ultimate modding companion, built for everyone.

The Stardew Valley Accessible Mod Manager is a powerful, streamlined tool designed specifically for players who rely on screen readers or keyboard-only navigation. It removes the barriers to modding, allowing you to focus on what matters most: your farm.

### Key Features:

*   **Designed for Accessibility**: Built from the ground up with full keyboard control and screen reader compatibility. Every action, status update, and notification is announced through your preferred screen reader (NVDA, JAWS, or SAPI).
*   **Audio-First Feedback**: Features unique audio themes and sound cues. Know instantly when a mod is enabled, disabled, or when an update is available through high-quality sound effects.
*   **One-Click Nexus Integration**: Browse, search, and download mods directly from Nexus Mods within the app. Full support for "Mod Manager Download" links ensures a seamless installation experience.
*   **Smart Update Management**: Automatically checks your entire mod list for updates and lets you update everything with a single keystroke (Premium).
*   **Total Safety & Backups**: Never worry about breaking your game. The manager automatically creates zip backups of your mods before any changes, allowing for instant restoration.
*   **Mod Profiles**: Switch between different mod setups for different playthroughs in seconds. Profiles even remember your favorite audio themes!
*   **SMAPI Log Integration**: Built-in log analyzer that helps you find and fix errors. It can even suggest missing dependencies and search for them automatically.
*   **Smart Launching**: Launches your game via SMAPI and provides audio feedback when the game is loading, running, and closed.

### Why Choose This Manager?

Most mod managers are visually heavy and difficult to navigate with assistive technology. The Stardew Valley Accessible Mod Manager changes that by providing a high-signal, low-noise environment that puts the power of modding back into the hands of the community.
